Broad Market View

The Year-End Lull: The Indian equity markets witnessed a "year-end lull" this week, with the Nifty failing to hold the psychological 26,000 mark. The week was characterized by FII (Foreign Institutional Investor) outflows, as foreigners dumped a record number of Indian bonds and equities to book profits before the calendar turn.

Volatility remains the key theme. While the IT sector faced headwinds from global cues, the Steel sector saw a brief rally of up to 5% triggered by the imposition of a 3-year safeguard duty.

Theme of the Week: The IPO Gold Rush

FY2025: A Historic Year for Indian Primary Markets.
The biggest financial story as we close the year is the unprecedented liquidity event in the primary markets. India's IPO market hit a record ₹1.95 Lakh Crore in fundraising for FY2025, eclipsing all previous records.

However, the devil is in the details: nearly 65% of this capital raised was via Offer for Sale (OFS), meaning money went to exiting investors (PE/VCs) rather than into the companies for growth.

Deals Under Watch

  • Zepto: The quick-commerce giant continues its aggressive capitalization, leading 2025's growth-stage rounds with a fresh $450 Million raise.
  • Bagmane Prime Office REIT: Blackstone-backed entity has filed for a $445 Million IPO, signaling continued institutional bullishness on Indian commercial real estate.
  • CoreEL Technologies: Defense electronics firm secured $72 Million, underscoring the "Make in India" defense theme.
