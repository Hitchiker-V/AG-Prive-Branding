Broad Market View

The Year-End Lull: The Indian equity markets witnessed a "year-end lull" this week, with the Nifty failing to hold the psychological 26,000 mark. The week was characterized by FII (Foreign Institutional Investor) outflows, as foreigners dumped a record number of Indian bonds and equities to book profits before the calendar turn.

Volatility remains the key theme. While the IT sector faced headwinds from global cues, the Steel sector saw a brief rally of up to 5% triggered by the imposition of a 3-year safeguard duty. Domestic Institutional Investors (DIIs) have continued to support the market, absorbing much of the foreign selling pressure.

Theme of the Week: The IPO Gold Rush

FY2025: A Historic Year for Indian Primary Markets.

The biggest financial story as we close the year is the unprecedented liquidity event in the primary markets. India's IPO market hit a record ₹1.95 Lakh Crore in fundraising for FY2025, eclipsing all previous records.

However, the devil is in the details: nearly 65% of this capital raised was via Offer for Sale (OFS), meaning money went to exiting investors (PE/VCs) rather than into the companies for growth. As we move into 2026, the focus shifts from "listing pop" to "earnings sustainability."

Deals Under Watch

  • Zepto: The quick-commerce giant continues its aggressive capitalization, leading 2025's growth-stage rounds with a fresh $450 Million raise.
  • Bagmane Prime Office REIT: Blackstone-backed entity has filed for a $445 Million IPO, signaling continued institutional bullishness on Indian commercial real estate.
  • HUL & Minimalist: In a major D2C consolidation, HUL's acquisition of Minimalist for $350 Million highlights the exit potential for premium niche brands.
  • CoreEL Technologies: Defense electronics firm secured $72 Million, underscoring the "Make in India" defense theme.
 

Expert's Opinion

Q&A with Neelesh Surana, CIO at Mirae Asset Investment Managers

Q: With Nifty hovering near 26k, are valuations stretched for 2026?

"Valuations are now more reasonable—neither cheap nor excessive. The returns in 2026 will be strictly earnings-driven. We are moving away from the liquidity-fueled rallies of 2024-25."

Q: Where do you see the opportunity in the coming year?

"While FY26 earnings growth might remain modest, the setup for FY27 appears materially stronger. We prefer a staggered deployment of capital into large-caps and select mid-caps that have consolidated."
